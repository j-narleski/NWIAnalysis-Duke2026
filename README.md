# National Wetlands Inventory (NWI) Analysis of Clean Water Act Section 404 Permits in Texas
## By Jaclyn Narleski, Master of Environmental Management '26
## Duke University, Nicholas School of the Environment
## Spring 2026

The R Script created for this section of the analysis in *Replicate and Expand Ongoing Research on WOTUS Over Time: An Examination of Potential Impacts on Wetlands/Streams Over Different Definitions of Waters of the United States (WOTUS)* was in collaboration with the Environmental Policy Innovation Center (EPIC) and Chris Samoray, PhD Candidate at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. The purpose of this analysis was to quantify the permitted impacts under the Clean Water Act's Section 404 permitting program in the state of Texas using U.S. Army Corps of Engineers permit data and the National Wetlands Inventory (NWI). The methods and related code this analysis was based on will be posted publicly in Samoray's dissertation chapter "What Sackett Would Have Cost: A Counterfactual Analysis of Compensatory Mitigation for Wetlands in North Carolina" (cited below). 

Christopher Samoray, *What Sackett Would Have Cost: A Counterfactual Analysis of Compensatory Mitigation for Wetlands in North Carolina* (unpublished doctoral dissertation chapter, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, forthcoming 2026).
